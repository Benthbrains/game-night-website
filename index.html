Chess Questions üé≤

  1. What move would you play as White to checkmate in 2 moves? Position FEN: 5rk1/ppp4Q/P7/8/8/8/PPPPPPP1/RNBQKBNR w - - 0 1 (View diagram: Lichess Analysis)
    1. Qh8+ Kxh8 2. Qh7#
  2. Identify if the following position is check, checkmate, or stalemate. Position FEN: rnbqkbnr/pppp1ppp/8/4p3/6Pq/5p2/PPPPPPPP/RNBQKBNR w KQkq - 0 3 (View: Lichess) Checkmate (similar to Fool's Mate variant, white cannot move without the king being captured next turn).
  3. List three openings you can play as White and explain the main ideas. 1. King's Pawn Opening (1. e4): Controls the center, opens lines for queen and bishop, aggressive play. 2. Queen's Pawn Opening (1. d4): Controls center, supports c4 for Queen's Gambit, solid. 3. English Opening (1. c4): Flank opening, controls d5, flexible development.
  4. Explain the difference between a pin, a fork, and a skewer. Pin: A piece is pinned to a more valuable piece behind it, cannot move without exposing the valuable piece. Fork: A piece attacks two or more enemy pieces simultaneously. Skewer: An attack on two pieces in a line, where the front piece is valuable and moves, exposing the back one.
  5. What is the best move for Black in this scenario? Position FEN: r3k3/8/8/8/8/8/8/K7 b - - 0 1 (View: Lichess) Ra1# (the rook moves to a1, checkmating the white king as the path is clear and the king has no escape).
  6. What piece is worth 3 points, and which is worth 5 points? Bishop and knight are worth 3 points each; rook is worth 5 points.
  7. Define castling and explain when it is legal. Castling is a special move where the king and rook switch positions to protect the king. It is legal if neither has moved before, no pieces between them, the king is not in check, and the squares the king passes are not attacked.
  8. What is en passant, and when can it be performed? En passant is a special pawn capture where a pawn that has just advanced two squares can be captured by an adjacent enemy pawn as if it had advanced one square. It can only be performed on the next move immediately after the two-square advance.
  9. Name two famous chess grandmasters and one of their achievements. Magnus Carlsen: Current world champion, held the title since 2013. Garry Kasparov: World champion from 1985 to 2000, known for beating IBM's Deep Blue in 1996 (but losing in 1997).
  10. Explain the purpose of a gambit in chess. A gambit is a chess opening where a player sacrifices material (usually a pawn) to achieve an advantage in development, position, or attack initiative.
